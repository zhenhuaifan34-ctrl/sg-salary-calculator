# Singapore CPF Contribution Guide 2026 The Central Provident Fund (CPF) is Singapore's mandatory social security savings scheme. Both employers and employees contribute a percentage of monthly wages to CPF accounts. ## What Is CPF? CPF helps Singapore citizens and permanent residents save for: * Retirement * Healthcare * Housing * Insurance protection CPF contributions are deducted automatically from monthly salaries. ## CPF Contribution Rates in 2026 CPF contribution rates depend on: * Age * Citizenship status * Monthly wages For employees aged 55 and below: | Contributor | Rate | | ---------------------- | ---- | | Employee | 20% | | Employer | 17% | | Total CPF Contribution | 37% | ## CPF Contribution Example Monthly Salary: SGD 4,000 Employee CPF: SGD 4,000 × 20% = SGD 800 Employer CPF: SGD 4,000 × 17% = SGD 680 Total CPF Contribution: SGD 1,480 Take-home salary: SGD 4,000 − SGD 800 = SGD 3,200 ## Ordinary Wage Ceiling CPF contributions are subject to the Ordinary Wage ceiling set by CPF Board. Employees earning above the ceiling may not contribute CPF on the excess amount. Always check the latest CPF Board announcements for updates. ## Why CPF Matters CPF affects: * Take-home pay * Retirement savings * Housing affordability * Healthcare funding Understanding CPF contributions helps employees plan their finances more effectively. ## Final Thoughts CPF is one of the most important parts of Singapore's employment system. Whether you are a local employee or an employer, understanding CPF contribution rates helps you estimate salary costs and net income accurately.

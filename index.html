Ultimate Guide to Singapore Overtime (OT) Pay & CPF Rules 2026

Are you working in Singapore and wondering if your overtime pay is calculated correctly? This Singapore OT Calculator 2026 is designed to help employees under the Employment Act determine their rightful compensation including CPF contributions.

Who is covered under the Employment Act for OT?

According to the Ministry of Manpower (MOM) guidelines, you are entitled to overtime pay if you are:

  • A non-workman (office staff) earning up to S$2,600 basic monthly salary.
  • A workman (manual labor) earning up to S$4,500 basic monthly salary.

How is OT Calculated in Singapore?

The standard formula for overtime pay in Singapore is:
(12 × Monthly Basic Salary) / (52 × 44) × OT Rate × Hours Worked
Where the OT rate is 1.5x for normal working days and 2.0x for rest days or public holidays.

Frequently Asked Questions (2026 Update)

Q1: What is the CPF Ordinary Wage (OW) ceiling in 2026?
As of 1 January 2026, the CPF OW ceiling has increased to $8,000 per month.

Q2: Who is eligible for Overtime (OT) pay in Singapore?
Under Part IV of the Employment Act, you are eligible if you are a non-workman earning up to $2,600 or a workman earning up to $4,500.

Q3: What is the new Local Qualifying Salary (LQS) for July 2026?
From 1 July 2026, the LQS for full-time local employees will increase from $1,600 to $1,800.

Q4: How do the 2026 CPF changes affect my take-home pay?
If your monthly salary is between $7,400 and $8,000, your take-home pay will decrease slightly because more is contributed to CPF.

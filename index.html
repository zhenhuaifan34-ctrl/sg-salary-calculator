      Ultimate Guide to Singapore Overtime (OT) Pay & CPF Rules 2026

      Are you working in Singapore and wondering if your overtime pay is calculated correctly? This Singapore OT Calculator 2026 is designed to help employees under the Employment Act determine their rightful compensation including CPF contributions and Resignation Encashment options.

      ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (2026 MOM Rules)

      Q1: What is the maximum statutory overtime (OT) hours allowed per month in Singapore?

      According to the Ministry of Manpower (MOM), an employee is strictly not permitted to work more than 72 hours of overtime a month, unless an explicit exemption waiver has been granted to the employer.

      Q2: Does my 2026 overtime pay attract Central Provident Fund (CPF) contributions?

      Yes, under the CPF Act, overtime pay is classified as part of your total monthly wages. Therefore, it is fully subject to both statutory employee deductions and employer matching contribution rates.

      Q3: What is the new CPF Ordinary Wage (OW) ceiling for 2026?

      As of 1 January 2026, the CPF Ordinary Wage (OW) monthly ceiling has officially completed its phased hike, settling at a maximum cap of $8,000 SGD per month.

      Q4: How is statutory annual leave encashment calculated upon resignation?

      Under Section 28 of the Employment Act, it must be computed using the Gross Rate of Pay formula: (12 × Monthly Basic Salary) / (52 × Working Days Per Week) × Unused Leaves.
